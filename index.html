02 Expertise and Philosophy

All Of My Secrets

  1. I only wear as many hats as possible

    Doing one thing all the time is boring. Every part of the process one learns helps the rest that much more. Design, code, content, research, testing, and constant iteration. To everyone else, these seem to be separate concepts, but to me it's all part of building a successful system to deliver value, to the right people, at the right time, in the right way.

  2. Proof over pudding

    I like to hit the ground fast. The quicker I can create something real and palpable, the sooner I have proof it works. Anything before that is noise, theory, or conjecture. High level thought is useful to see the forest, and is easier to grok on paper, but there are a lot of trees to plant.

  3. Precedent matters

    After a few millennia of commerce, hundreds of years of marketing, and 70 years of computing, humans have learned a thing or two about processing value. Even if you're dealing with something brand new, it's possible the solution to the problem at hand exists in the past. It just needs to be translated. Then again, there's no use putting wheels on a hovercraft, just because you have them laying around.

  4. If you don't do your best, you'll live to regret it

    The double edged sword of software is that it never _has_ to be finished. I'd rather take the time and effort to make something incredible now — rather than taking the risk of never having the chance.

03 Client List

Public engagements

The rules have been simple: show up every day, always improve the client's condition, and be ruthlessly honest with everyone.

  • HBO

    I've worked on at least four different primetime television series for them throughout the past decade, doing graphic design and on-screen interactive. I'm also the unofficial typography consultant for growing percentage of their executives.

  • Dinner Lab

    I was their first technical hire outside of the original partners. I built out a custom design system for their client facing interface and marketing pages. While the company no longer exists, in its heyday the company was active in over 30 cities across the country.

  • nclud

    As a Front End Developer, I worked on projects for Tesla Motors, George Washington University, Wingate Hughes Architecture, and General Assembly, among many others. Collaborating with some amazing peers, I was also the catalyst behind their latest rebrand.

  • Agora

    I was hired as a front end developer for their largest property, Stansberry & Associates. I refactored a legacy (read: horrifying) codebase and built two new design systems, but my crowning achievement was a small improvement to an onboarding form that resulted in an additional ~$200,000 in revenue, practically overnight.

  • Schindler Group

    I designed an updated version of their interactive elevator cab designer. Although there were heavy constraints and requirements, I was able to create something that got the job done gracefully. I have a deep appreciation for "boring" functional design, and this was a great opportunity to do precisely that.

  • Nick Noble Works

    I know it's weird to put myself down as a client, but I'm very proud of the fact that I've built a sustainable and successful freelance practice. Since I'm applying for a job I think this is great artifact to look at: not only do I know how to do the work, but I have first hand understanding of the underlying business problems and constraints that come into play.
