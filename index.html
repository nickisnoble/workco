01 Expertise and Philosophy

A Few Of My Secrets

  1. Boxes suck

    I can design, code both ends, write the copy, strategize a launch, and make it actually happen. I'm not saying I'm the best at any of these, but I'm the best (that I know of) at all of them put together. I want a place to work where I'm encouraged to do more than one thing, leveraging the sum of the parts.

  2. 10,000 feet is great if you never want to touch the ground

    High level thought is fantastic for getting other people to buy into your ideas. But when it comes time to actually build something, one needs to get concrete. I like to make decisions quickly and test rather than talk — the faster you can put something into the field, the faster you know it works.

  3. The right tool for the job

    Humans have been processing value for a long time. In software, we get to make things that have never been done before— but it's possible an analogous solution still exists in the past. It just needs to be translated. On the other hand, there's no use putting wheels on a hovercraft, just because we have them laying around.

  4. If you don't do your best, you'll live to regret it

    The double edged sword of software is that it never has to be finished. I'd rather take the time and effort to make something incredible now — rather than taking the risk of never having the chance.

02 Client List

Public engagements

The rules have been simple: show up every day, always improve the client's condition, and be ruthlessly honest with everyone.

  • HBO

    I've worked on at least four different primetime television series for them throughout the past decade, doing graphic design and on-screen interactive. I'm also the unofficial typography consultant for a growing percentage of their executives.

  • Dinner Lab

    I was their first technical hire outside of the original partners. I created a custom design system for their client facing interface and marketing pages. While the company no longer exists, in its heyday it was active in over 30 cities across the country.

  • nclud

    As a Front End Developer, I worked on projects for Tesla Motors, George Washington University, Wingate Hughes Architecture, and General Assembly, among many others. Collaborating with some amazing peers, through an internal talk I was also the catalyst behind their latest rebrand.

  • Agora

    I was hired as a front end developer for their largest property, Stansberry & Associates. I refactored a legacy (read: horrifying) codebase and created two new design systems. My crowning achievement here was a small improvement to an onboarding form that resulted in an additional ~$200,000 in revenue, practically overnight.

  • Schindler Group

    I designed an updated version of their interactive elevator cab designer. Although there were heavy constraints and requirements, I was able to create something that got the job done gracefully. I have a deep appreciation for "boring" functional design, and this was a great opportunity to do precisely that.

  • Nick Noble Works

    I know it's weird to put myself down as a client, but I'm very proud of the fact that I've built a sustainable and successful freelance practice. Since I'm applying for a job I think this is great artifact to look at: not only do I know how to do the work, but I have first hand understanding of the underlying business problems and constraints that come into play.
